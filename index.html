Interesting facts about camels-

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Interesting facts about chameleons-

  • Chameleons have eyes that can swivel.
  • The panther chameleon changes its color to yellow or red when it's angry.
  • When a chameleon is cold, it will become darker in color.

Interesting facts about pigeon-

  • Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  • Pigeons are herbivores (plant-eaters). Their diet consists of seeds, fruit and various plants.
  • Pigeons were used as mail carriers during the First and Second World War. They saved numerous lives by delivering information on the enemy's fire.

Interesting facts about bears-

  • Giant pandas (often referred to as simply “pandas”) are black and white bears.
  • Giant pandas grow to between 1.2m and 1.5m, and weigh between 75kg and 135kg.
  • Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!

Interesting facts about bears-

  • Bears Have Lived on Earth for 38 Million Years.
  • There Are Only 8 Species of Bear.
  • The Last Bears in Africa Went Extinct in the Late 1800s.

Interesting facts about flying cicadas-

  • Cicadas are insects.
  • Cicada in Latin means tree cricket.
  • There are over 2,000 types of cicadas.
